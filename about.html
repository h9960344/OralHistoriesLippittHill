About


In 1959, Lippitt Hill, one of the oldest black neighborhoods in Providence — called by locals at the time "The East Side"— was slated for demolition. The result of racially motivated urban renewal, the project displaced hundreds of people, closed their businesses, and largely erased the vibrant community that once existed there. In 2017, Stages of Freedom brought together members of the community to tell stories of life on Lippitt Hill — their words can be viewed here.
About this Project: This site was created to preserve and share the stories of residents from the Lippitt Hill neighborhood, connecting them to the physical locations where they took place. The oral histories collected here were recorded during community events organized by Stages of Freedom in 2017, under a special grant from the Heritage Harbor Foundation. Acknowledgments: This project builds upon the important work done by Stages of Freedom, who organized and conducted the original oral history interviews with former Lippitt Hill residents.
